About

AI security researcher, red teamer, and PhD candidate. Responsible AI is my core lens for understanding security, alignment, and safer AI development.

Who I Am

I'm an AI security researcher and red teamer with a focus on identifying vulnerabilities in AI systems before they cause harm. My core lens is Responsible AI—a philosophy that positions security, alignment, and ethical development as foundational to building trustworthy systems. Rather than treating these as afterthoughts, I see them as essential design principles.

My work combines rigorous security engineering with emerging machine learning concerns to help organizations build safer, more reliable AI. I'm a PhD candidate at the University of Catania, researching adversarial robustness and safety mechanisms in large language models. Simultaneously, I work in industry on practical red-teaming initiatives and security assessments.

My Approach

I believe AI security isn't just about finding bugs—it's about understanding systemic risks through the lens of Responsible AI. My methodology combines:

  • Responsible AI as Foundation: Every red-teaming exercise, every security assessment, and every recommendation is grounded in principles of fairness, transparency, accountability, and ethical development.
  • Adversarial Thinking: Approaching systems as a potential attacker would, but always in service of making AI safer and more aligned with human values.
  • Rigor: Grounding red-teaming exercises in structured threat models, documented methodologies, and evidence-based security practices.
  • Collaboration: Working closely with engineers, researchers, and policy makers to ensure security, ethics, and alignment are woven throughout the development lifecycle.
  • Continuous Learning: Staying current with emerging attack vectors, model capabilities, responsible AI frameworks, and regulatory developments.

What Drives My Work

The rapid advancement of AI systems has outpaced our understanding of their risks and societal impact. I'm driven by the belief that Responsible AI—approached seriously through security research, red-teaming, and rigorous evaluation—can help bridge this gap. Every red-team exercise, every publication, every conversation with a team about their threat model and ethical alignment makes AI systems safer and more trustworthy for everyone.

I'm committed to making AI security and responsible development accessible and actionable—not just for security specialists, but for all teams involved in building AI systems. Clear communication about risks, alignment challenges, and ethical considerations is as important as identifying them.

Beyond Work
