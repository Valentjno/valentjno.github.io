Research

Advancing Responsible AI through security research, adversarial testing, and rigorous evaluation of AI systems.

Overview

My research is driven by a core conviction: Responsible AI isn't aspirational—it's essential engineering practice. I focus on identifying vulnerabilities, understanding attack surfaces, and building safety mechanisms into AI systems from the ground up. This work spans academic research, industry collaboration, and practical red-teaming initiatives.

Every research project is grounded in the question: How do we make AI systems more secure, aligned, and trustworthy? Whether it's adversarial robustness, prompt injection vulnerabilities, or safety mechanisms in large language models, the goal is always to advance both security understanding and responsible AI practice.

OWASP AI Exchange

Contributor to OWASP AI Exchange

OWASP AI Exchange is a global initiative bringing together the world's foremost AI security experts to establish comprehensive, practical guidance for securing AI systems. As a contributor, I work alongside leading researchers and practitioners to develop security controls, threat models, and best practices for responsible AI development.

My contributions focus on adversarial robustness, prompt injection vulnerabilities, and safety mechanisms in large language models. Through OWASP AI Exchange, we're building the security reference architecture for a more trustworthy AI ecosystem. This is Responsible AI in action—translating research into actionable guidance that organizations can implement immediately.

Research Focus Areas

Adversarial Robustness

Understanding how AI systems fail under adversarial conditions, and designing defenses that are both effective and practical for real-world deployment.

Prompt Injection & Manipulation

Identifying vulnerabilities in language model interfaces where adversarial inputs can manipulate behavior, and developing robust mitigation strategies.

Safety Mechanisms & Alignment

Evaluating the effectiveness of safety mechanisms, red-teaming approaches for alignment testing, and building systems that behave as intended even under pressure.

Red-Teaming Methodology

Developing structured, reproducible red-teaming frameworks that organizations can use to assess AI risks before deployment, ensuring rigor and consistency.

Academic Work

As a PhD candidate at the University of Catania, my research explores the intersection of adversarial robustness and AI safety. My work combines classical security thinking with emerging concerns specific to machine learning and large language models.

My academic research agenda includes:

  • Adversarial Robustness in LLMs: Understanding how language models respond to adversarial inputs, and designing defenses that preserve utility while improving security.
  • Prompt Injection Vulnerabilities: Systematically mapping the attack surface of language model APIs and building practical defenses.
  • Responsible AI Frameworks: Evaluating how security, fairness, transparency, and accountability can be integrated throughout the AI development lifecycle.
  • Red-Team Effectiveness: Measuring what makes red-teaming exercises effective, and how they can be scaled across organizations.

Publications & Contributions

I publish regularly on AI security topics, contributing to both peer-reviewed venues and open-source security frameworks. My publications focus on bridging the gap between academic research and practical security engineering.

Recent work covers adversarial testing methodologies, prompt injection at scale, and the role of red-teaming in responsible AI development. I'm committed to making security research accessible—not just for researchers, but for all practitioners involved in building AI systems.

Following the open science principle, I contribute to collaborative initiatives like OWASP AI Exchange, where practical guidance can be developed and refined through community expertise.

Current Research Direction

As AI systems become more capable and more widely deployed, the security and safety challenges become more urgent. My current focus is on:

  • Scalable Red-Teaming: How can organizations systematically evaluate AI risks at scale, without requiring specialized expertise?
  • Adversarial Resilience: What does true robustness look like for language models, and how can it be achieved without sacrificing capability?
  • Safety-by-Design: How can Responsible AI principles be embedded into system architecture, not bolted on afterward?
  • Measurable Security: Developing quantifiable metrics for AI security that practitioners can track and improve over time.

Collaboration & Community

I believe that advancing AI security requires collaboration across academia, industry, and policy. Whether it's contributing to OWASP initiatives, collaborating with research groups, or advising organizations on their security posture, I'm committed to raising the bar for responsible AI practices.
